Sir Paul Scoon
Sir Paul was born on 4 July 1935 in Gouyave, He attended St. John's Anglican School and then the Grenada Boys' Secondary School. Sir Paul then received an external degree from the University of London before going on to study at the University of Leeds, England, and gaining an M.Ed. at the University of Toronto in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He returned to Grenada to teach at the Grenada Boys' Secondary School. Following a career rising from Chief Education Officer to finally becoming Secretary to the Cabinet, the head of Grenada's Civil Service. He was awarded the OBE in 1970 and in 1973 he returned to London to fill the post of Deputy Director of the Commonwealth Foundation.
In 1978, Sir Paul was appointed Governor-General of Grenada by Queen Elizabeth II, on the advice of Prime Minister Sir Eric Gairy. However, the following year, the New Jewel Movement—led by Maurice Bishop and his former pupils, Bernard Coard and Hudson Austin—overthrew the current government led by Sir Eric Gairy.
Initially arrested by the militants, Sir Paul was soon released and he and Maurice Bishop managed to maintain a semi-normal working relationship, despite the fact that Sir Paul strongly opposed Maurice Bishop's Provisional Revolutionary Government. Bishop agreed to retain Grenada's status as a constitutional monarchy and the position of governor-general to represent the monarch, Queen Elizabeth II. Sir Paul was happy to remain in office, providing a degree of stability in the situation, despite irritation at the curtailment of some of his privileges. He played tennis with Bishop and maintained his reputation for discretion.
Sir Paul published a book entitled Survival for Service that provided a personal account of his experiences as governor-general. In it, he denied writing the 24 October letter, saying it had been delivered to him on 27 October, after he was rescued. He did, however, confirm that he had asked for U.S. and Caribbean intervention. The accuracy of some of this book has been questioned by Richard Hart and Jeremy Taylor.
Sir Paul Scoon died on 2 September 2013 and made request that he be buried in the town of Gouyave the place of his birth.
